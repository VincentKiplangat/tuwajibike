Who Are We?

Welcome to the Tuwajibike Workers Consortium

A dedicated nonprofit organization committed to championing the rights and welfare of workers, both locally and abroad. Our organization was founded on the principles of charity, empowerment, education, and legal awareness, with a specific focus on addressing the endemic abuse of workers in the Gulf nations and other regions around the world. We believe that every worker deserves dignity, respect, and a safe working environment.

Our Vision
We envision a community-based organization that empowers members and the broader community to actively participate in socioeconomic activities through sustainable capacity building and training programs. By fostering an environment of mutual support and growth, we aim to uplift the living standards of all our members and the community at large.
Mission
Our mission is to foster a holistic approach in uplifting the living standards of the entire community through innovative and practical actions aimed at improving socioeconomic and environmental conditions. We strive to create a society where every individual has the opportunity to thrive, supported by a network that champions their rights and well-being.

How We Help The community

Counseling and Support Groups

At Tuwajibike Workers Consortium, we understand the importance of mental health and emotional well-being. Our counseling services provide professional support for individuals dealing with various challenges, including mental health issues, addiction, grief, and domestic violence. In addition, we facilitate support groups that bring together people facing similar difficulties, offering a safe space for sharing experiences and fostering mutual support. These groups help build a sense of community and understanding, ensuring that no one has to face their struggles alone.

Youth Employment Services

We are committed to empowering young people by enhancing their employability and career prospects. Our youth employment services include comprehensive job training programs that equip participants with the necessary skills for today’s job market. We assist with resume building and interview preparation, ensuring young job seekers present themselves effectively to potential employers. Furthermore, our job placement services connect youths with employment opportunities, helping them secure meaningful and sustainable work. We aim to support young individuals throughout their employment journey, from training to placement.

Advocacy and Legal Aid

Tuwajibike Workers Consortium is dedicated to advocating for social justice and providing essential legal support. Our services include assistance with legal issues such as immigration, housing rights, and employment disputes. We also actively work to promote policies and practices that address social inequalities and injustices through lobbying and public awareness campaigns. Our advocacy efforts aim to create a more equitable society, ensuring that the rights of all individuals are protected and upheld.

Read More
Financial Assistance

Financial Assistance: Financial stability is crucial for overall well-being, and we offer various forms of financial assistance to support individuals and families in need. Our services include direct financial support through grants or emergency funds to help those facing financial hardships. Additionally, we provide budgeting and financial planning education, empowering individuals to manage their finances effectively and plan for a secure future. Our goal is to alleviate financial stress and promote economic self-sufficiency.

Read More
Emergency Relief

In times of crisis, Tuwajibike Workers Consortium is dedicated to providing immediate and effective relief. Our emergency relief services are geared towards delivering aid in the aftermath of natural disasters, including food, water, shelter, and medical care. We also offer crisis intervention support during events such as pandemics or economic downturns, helping affected individuals and communities recover and rebuild. Our prompt and compassionate response ensures that those in need receive the support necessary to navigate and overcome challenging situations.

Health and Wellness Programs

Promoting a healthy lifestyle is essential for overall well-being, and our health and wellness programs aim to support individuals in achieving this goal. We provide various services, including health education workshops, fitness classes, and access to preventative healthcare. Our programs also focus on nutrition, offering guidance on healthy eating habits and managing chronic conditions. By encouraging physical and mental health, we aim to improve the quality of life for our community members.

What We Are Doing

We've Done Lot's of Awesome Events

Nourishing the Future In a heartfelt initiative, Tuwajibike Workers Consortium provided nutritious lunches to needy students, ensuring they have the energy and focus to excel in their studies. This event aimed to support their educational journey by addressing one of their most basic needs.
Dignity and Health: Sanitary Towels Distribution Addressing a critical need, Tuwajibike Workers Consortium distributed sanitary towels to girls who cannot afford them. This initiative aimed to promote health, dignity, and uninterrupted education for girls, ensuring they have the necessary resources to manage their periods safely and confidently.
Empowering Dreams: Embrace Your Future Tuwajibike Workers Consortium hosted an inspiring session for children, encouraging them to embrace their future with optimism and resilience. Through motivational talks and interactive activities, the children were taught the importance of perseverance and self-belief, no matter their current circumstances.
Knowledge is Power: Children's Rights and Responsibilities Tuwajibike Workers Consortium conducted an educational session on children's rights and responsibilities. The children were taught about their legal rights, the importance of understanding them, and their responsibilities as young citizens. This event aimed to empower children with knowledge, fostering a sense of responsibility and awareness about their roles in society.
